While waiting at an Airport Gate or while having a coffee at Starbucks have you ever wondered that the person, the business lead or the opportunity that you are looking for may be right there in that group? What if you could connect with them and have a 5 minute face to face meeting and share contact information to follow up?

wifi1.com enables you to do just that... in real time ...create a Network at your location or join one if one has already been created by another subscriber. Login via Facebook, LinkedIn or Google. A few keywords in your profile will let others see your background and goals and connect with you.

Using the ubiquitous of wireless network and your smartphone or tablet you can broaden your personal network with new contacts and make your waiting time more productive.

Wifi1.com is a startup based in San Diego, California that is looking for funding to further develop and test this idea.

There are over 30,000 commercial flights that originate in the US ( ~50,000 worldwide) every day and over half a million US passengers use air travel, a large percentage of them for business trips.

Wifi1.com provides a platform for its users to connect with other co passengers while they are waiting at the Gate or during flight. With the availability of the Internet on board the aircraft, users can have real time communications regardless of where they are seated.

Starbucks: Wifi1.com has a database of over 10,000 Starbucks locations. Many of these locations are used by professionals as a meeting place and to conduct business. The wifi1.com platform allows users to connect with others at these locations based upon their profiles and keywords.

Conference Centers: Another venue where professionals meet for a limited time are for business and professional conferences. People can use wifi1.com to connect with other professionals who may be there from all over the world and have a face to face meeting at no additonal cost.

FAQ

Will it work with wifi networks on the plane?
Yes, the networks created on the Gate can be continued provided there is access to wifi on the aircraft. In any case the network can be accessed from the my networks tab at any time after the network has timed out.
Is there a charge or subscription for this service?
This is a beta test site which is being evaluated for the market response. Decisions for revenue models will be made after the evaluation phase.
